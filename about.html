Our story

A gallery
without
walls

Founded in 2022 in Bengaluru, Krira Gallery began with a single belief — that extraordinary art should not be confined to geography, to privilege, or to the physical limits of a room with four walls.

How Krira
came to be

Krira Gallery was founded by Priyanka Singh, a collector and former art educator who spent a decade watching extraordinary work go unseen — not for lack of quality, but for lack of access. The gallery's name is drawn from the Sanskrit word कृत — "made with intention."

From a small studio in Indiranagar, Bengaluru, Krira began representing six artists. Within two years, it had facilitated over 320 collections across 12 countries, entirely online, with zero gallery walls.

We believe the relationship between artist and collector should be direct, transparent, and long-lasting. We do not chase trends. We do not rush artists. We invest in the long arc of a practice.

"Art does not need a room. It needs the right light, and the right person standing in it."
— Priyanka Singh, Founder

Our curatorial approach is deliberate and slow. We rarely add new artists to the programme — when we do, it is because we have spent months, sometimes years, following a practice and waiting for the right moment to introduce it to our collectors.

We are not a marketplace. We are a gallery — one that happens to exist everywhere at once.

What we stand for

01
Artist-first, always
Our artists set their prices. We support their practice on their timeline, not ours. Long-term relationships over short-term sales — this is the Krira principle.
02
Radical transparency
Every collector knows exactly where a work came from, who made it, and what a fair price looks like. We publish our commission structure openly.
03
Curation without compromise
We add one or two artists per year. Every work in the collection is something we would buy ourselves. Taste is a responsibility we take seriously.
